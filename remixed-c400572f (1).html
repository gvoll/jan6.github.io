What Happened

On January 6, 2021, a violent mob attacked the United States Capitol building during a joint session of Congress convened to certify the results of the 2020 presidential election. The attack represented one of the darkest days in American democracy.

Violence Against Law Enforcement

  • Approximately 80 U.S. Capitol Police officers and 60 Metropolitan Police Department officers were assaulted
  • Officers were beaten with weapons including flagpoles, stolen police shields, batons, baseball bats, and axes
  • Chemical irritants and sprays were used against defending officers
  • Officers were crushed in doorways and tunnels as they tried to hold back the mob
  • Five police officers died in the aftermath of the attacks, including Officer Brian Sicknick
  • Several officers who responded during the attack later died by suicide
"There's a common thread of emotions amongst many officers who were defending the Capitol on January 6, 2021, which is that we all thought we were going to die."
— Capitol Police Officer testimony

  • Windows and doors throughout the Capitol were smashed to gain entry
  • Historic statues were damaged and covered in what appeared to be blood
  • Offices were ransacked and vandalized, including those of Speaker Nancy Pelosi
  • The Senate and House chambers were breached and occupied
  • A Confederate battle flag was carried through the Capitol Rotunda — the first time in history

  • The building was littered with debris, trash, and evidence of violence

The Weapons Used

Over 75 people were charged with entering restricted areas while armed with dangerous weapons, including:

  • Firearms and stun guns
  • Knives and axes
  • Baseball bats and batons
  • Flagpoles used as spears
  • Stolen police riot shields
  • Chemical sprays and bear spray
  • Crutches and makeshift weapons

The Breach

This was the first time the Capitol had been breached since the British attacked and burned the building in August 1814 during the War of 1812.

  • The attack disrupted the constitutional process of certifying a presidential election
  • Members of Congress and Vice President Mike Pence were evacuated to secure locations
  • Rioters came within 40 feet of Vice President Pence as he was being evacuated
  • The Capitol was under siege for approximately 4 hours before being secured
  • Congress returned later that evening to complete the certification of election results

Attempts to Rewrite History

In recent years, there have been coordinated efforts by some political figures to minimize, distort, or deny the severity of the January 6 attack:

  • Characterizing the violent mob as "peaceful protesters" or "tourists" despite extensive video evidence of assaults and destruction
  • Downplaying the injuries sustained by law enforcement officers who defended the Capitol
  • Suggesting the attack was orchestrated by government agents rather than Trump supporters, despite overwhelming evidence to the contrary
  • Proposing pardons for those convicted of violent crimes during the attack
  • Attempting to reframe rioters as "political prisoners" or "hostages" rather than individuals convicted in court
  • Dismissing the extensive Congressional investigation and court proceedings as politically motivated
These revisionist narratives contradict the testimony of police officers who were there, the convictions secured in federal court with overwhelming evidence, and the documented injuries and deaths that resulted from the attack.

The facts remain clear: January 6, 2021 was a violent attack on the U.S. Capitol that resulted in deaths, hundreds of injuries to law enforcement, millions in damages, and a disruption of the constitutional process. No amount of political rhetoric can change the documented reality of what occurred that day.

Remember

Five years later, we remember the courage of the law enforcement officers who defended our democracy, often at great personal cost. We remember the sanctity of the Capitol building as a symbol of American democracy. And we remember the importance of protecting our democratic institutions from violence and insurrection.

"Jan. 6 was a nightmare for me that nearly cost me my life… I have been sentenced to a lifetime of medical issues that include physical pain and mental and emotional distress."
— Capitol Police Officer

The information presented in this memorial is compiled from official government sources, court records, congressional investigations, and verified news reports:

  • U.S. Capitol Police: Reports on officer casualties, injuries, and damages to Capitol facilities.
  • Metropolitan Police Department: Documentation of officer assaults and injuries during the attack.
  • House Select Committee to Investigate the January 6th Attack: Final report and testimony from over 1,000 witnesses, including law enforcement officers, elected officials, and Trump administration members.
  • Architect of the Capitol: Official damage assessments and repair cost estimates for the Capitol building.
  • Federal Court Records: Thousands of pages of evidence, testimony, and judicial findings from criminal prosecutions.
  • Government Accountability Office (GAO): Cost estimates including security enhancements and investigations totaling approximately $2.7 billion.
  • Congressional Research Service: Analysis of the attack, its unprecedented nature in American history, and constitutional implications.
  • Video and Photographic Evidence: Thousands of hours of body camera footage, security camera recordings, and videos posted by participants themselves documenting the violence.

These facts are matters of public record, established through extensive investigations, sworn testimony, and criminal convictions in federal court. They represent the documented reality of what occurred on January 6, 2021.
